Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

This is authentic Italian spaghetti carbonara, prepared in the classic way — no cream whatsoever. Its rich, silky sauce comes entirely from eggs and cheese. A dish worthy of royalty, it’s a perfect example of how pure simplicity can deliver extraordinary results.

Ingredients

  • 175g / 6 oz guanciale (pancetta or block bacon)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 100g / 3.5 oz parmigiano reggiano, finely shredded
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 400g / 14 oz spaghetti
  • 1 tbsp cooking/kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup pasta cooking water
  • 1 garlic clove, finely minced

Garnish (optional)

  • Parsley
  • Parmigiano regianno

Steps

  1. Prepare the guanciale – Slice it into roughly 0.5 cm (about 1/5 inch) strips, then cut those into small batons.
  2. Make the carbonara base – In a large bowl, whisk the eggs and yolks until smooth. Add the cheese and pepper, mixing until fully combined.
  3. Cook the pasta – Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions.
  4. Save pasta water – Just before draining, scoop out about 1 cup of the starchy pasta water. Then drain the pasta.
  5. Crisp the guanciale – While the pasta cooks, place the guanciale in a cold nonstick pan and heat over medium-high. Cook for 4–5 minutes until browned and the fat has rendered. If adding garlic, stir it in during the final minute.
  6. Toss pasta in the pan – Add the hot spaghetti straight into the pan and coat it thoroughly in the rendered guanciale fat.
  7. Combine with the sauce – Transfer the pasta and the pan drippings into the bowl with the egg mixture. Pour in about 1/2 cup of the reserved pasta water and stir vigorously for a minute until the sauce becomes creamy and clings to the noodles.
  8. Serve – Dish the pasta into warm bowls. Serve right away, adding extra parmesan, black pepper, and chopped parsley if you like.
